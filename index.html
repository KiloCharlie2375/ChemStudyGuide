Matter & Measurement

  • Matter: anything with mass and volume
  • Physical/chemical properties, states, changes: (solid, liquid, gas, vapor), extensive vs. intensive
  • Mixtures: Homogeneous (solutions) vs. Heterogeneous
  • Separation Techniques: Filtration, Distillation, Chromatography
  • Scientific Method, Data: Quantitative vs Qualitative, accuracy vs. precision
  • Unit Conversions: Metric prefixes (kilo-, centi-, milli- etc.)
  • Temperature: K = °C + 273.15
  • Percent by Mass: mass element/mass compound × 100
Practice Q: Intensive vs Extensive?
Extensive: depends on amount (mass, volume). Intensive: independent (density, boiling point).

Atoms & The Periodic Table

  • Atomic Structure: protons (+), neutrons (0), electrons (–)
  • Periodic Table: arranged by atomic number (Moseley), groups = columns, periods = rows
  • Metals, Nonmetals, Metalloids: location, typical properties
  • Periodic Trends:
    • Atomic radius: increases ↓, decreases →
    • Ionization energy: decreases ↓, increases →
    • Electronegativity: decreases ↓, increases →
    • Electron Affinity: generally more negative →, less negative ↓
  • Groups:
    • 1A: Alkali metals (+1)
    • 2A: Alkaline earth metals (+2)
    • 7A: Halogens (–1)
    • 8A: Noble gases (0)
    • Transition: variable charges (exceptions: Zn +2, Ag +1)
Q: Why does atomic radius decrease across a period?
Increased nuclear charge pulls electrons closer.

Bonding & Structure

  • Ionic Bonds: transfer of electrons, cations (+) & anions (–), lattice, high mp/bp, conduct when molten
  • Covalent Bonds: sharing electrons, nonmetals, single/double/triple, VSEPR, polar/nonpolar, resonance
  • Metallic Bonds: "sea of electrons", conductivity, malleability
  • Alloys: substitutional/interstitial, improved properties
  • Lewis Structures: show valence e–, obey octet rule (exceptions: H, B, S, P...)
  • Polarity: ∆EN: nonpolar (0–0.2), polar covalent (0.3–1.7), ionic (>1.7)
  • IMFs: Dispersion < Dipole-dipole < Hydrogen bonding (N, O, F)
Example: Why is H₂O polar but CO₂ is nonpolar?
H₂O: bent (net dipole), CO₂: linear (dipoles cancel).

Gases & Gas Laws

  • Kinetic Molecular Theory: particles in constant, random motion; negligible volume; elastic collisions
  • Gas Laws:
    • Boyle’s: P₁V₁=P₂V₂
    • Charles’: V₁/T₁=V₂/T₂
    • Gay-Lussac’s: P₁/T₁=P₂/T₂
    • Avogadro’s: V₁/n₁=V₂/n₂ (at same T & P)
    • Ideal Gas: PV=nRT (R=0.0821 L·atm/mol·K)
    • Combined Gas: P₁V₁/T₁=P₂V₂/T₂
    • Dalton’s Law: P_total = P₁ + P₂ + ...
  • STP: 0°C, 1 atm, 1 mol gas = 22.4L
  • Real vs Ideal Gases: real gases deviate at low T, high P
  • Gas Pressure Units: 1 atm = 101.3 kPa = 760 mmHg = 14.7 psi
Sample: Find volume of 2 mol O₂ at 27°C, 2 atm.
V = nRT/P = (2)(0.0821)(300)/2 ≈ 24.6 L

Solutions & Molarity

  • Solutions: homogeneous mixtures, solute dissolved in solvent
  • Molarity (M): mol/L
  • Molality (m): mol/kg solvent
  • Dilutions: M₁V₁=M₂V₂
  • Percent by mass: (g solute/g solution) × 100
  • Colligative Properties: bp elevation ΔT_b = iK_b m, fp depression ΔT_f = iK_f m
  • Henry's Law: solubility of gas ∝ pressure
Practice: How much 12M HCl to make 500 mL of 2M HCl?
V₁ = (2)(0.5)/(12) = 0.083 L = 83 mL

Stoichiometry

  • Mole conversions: mass ↔ moles ↔ particles ↔ volume (gases @STP)
  • Percent Yield: actual/theoretical × 100
  • Limiting Reactant: smallest product amount (after conversion)
  • Empirical/Molecular Formula: lowest ratio (empirical); actual (molecular)
Sample: 10g H₂ + 80g O₂ → H₂O. What's limiting?
O₂ limiting; max H₂O = 80g/32 = 2.5 mol O₂ × 2 = 5 mol H₂O × 18 = 90g H₂O.

Equilibrium & Kinetics

  • Chemical Kinetics: speed of reaction (rate)
  • Collision Theory: particles must collide with right orientation and energy
  • Activation Energy (Ea): energy barrier
  • Catalysts: lower Ea, speed up reaction (not consumed)
  • Equilibrium: rates forward = reverse, concentrations constant
  • Keq = [products]/[reactants]: ignore pure solids/liquids
  • Ksp: solubility product, higher Ksp = more soluble
  • Le Chatelier’s Principle: system responds to minimize stress (concentration, T, P)
Example: What happens to equilibrium if [H₂] increases in N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃?
Shifts right (more NH₃).

Acids, Bases & pH

  • Acids: donate H⁺; Bases: accept H⁺
  • Strong/weak: extent of ionization
  • pH: -log[H⁺]; pOH: -log[OH⁻]
  • Kw: [H⁺][OH⁻]=1×10⁻¹⁴ at 25°C
  • Titration: MaVa = MbVb (for strong acid/base)
Sample: What is pH of 0.01 M HCl?
pH = -log(0.01) = 2
